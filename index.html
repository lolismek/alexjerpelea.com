// about

          

            I’m Alex Jerpelea, a rising sophomore at Columbia University studying CS & math.
            I have a ton of interests — from AI (deep learning, mechanistic interpretability,
            multi-agent systems, latent communication, machine translation) to efficient algorithms,
            systems design, UNIX, decentralized networks, and fun consumer apps. I grew up in
            Bucharest, Romania, where I spent most of high school doing competitive programming
            (see awards below), then got really into NLP, then ML as a whole, then startups, and so on.
            I’m just having fun learning as much as I can about all sorts of stuff.
          


          

            
currently

            

              I’m doing research at Columbia’s DAPLab, studying errors in multi-agent
              systems and efficient latent communication and memory storage to strengthen agents’
              “Theory of Mind”. I’m also studying the manifolds formed by concepts
              within Transformers, and self-experimenting with various hypotheses for how Transformers
              could be more efficient and deeper learners.
